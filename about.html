About Us

When we started Orlando WebCrafters, as team members of Orlando Science High School’s competitive math and programming teams, we knew our way around a 3.6 GHz 16 GB processor, and seemingly endless Diophantine equations, but Orlando WebCrafters presented us with a new type of challenge. Creating personalized custom websites, built to accomplish the goals and needs of our clients. What we soon learned, is that it takes a kind of dedication to detail that goes above and beyond skills we honed in the classroom. That’s why, since then, we’ve strived to bring you the best and broadest array of options, as we continue to learn and expand our library of capabilities every day tailoring to the intricate needs of each and every one of our clients.

Our Founders

Minh Le

Minh Le is a senior at Orlando Science High School. Over the past few years, he’s developed an interest and passion for programming from participating in his school’s FIRST Robotics Competition and FIRST Tech Challenge teams as Head of the Programming & Electrical Division. He’s also worked to organize and teach robotics and coding classes at the Orlando Science School’s Math Summer Camp and the Winter Park Public Library. Outside of robotics, he is the president and co-founder of his school’s Computer Science Honor Society and enjoys learning and applying new programming languages such as Python and C. Designing websites and apps is a challenge that Minh has gladly taken on, as he is always eager to expand the boundaries of his programming skills.

Wade Twyford

Wade Twyford is a senior at Orlando Science High School. Wade lives and breathes mathematics. He’s the founder of Problem Solved, a non-profit math tutoring service for students from elementary to high school. He’s also a gold medalist at the International Mathleague Tournament. His complete adoration for math led him to his second love, programming. “Computers and I get along very well,” he says, “it’s easy when you both speak the same language.” Since then, co-founding his school’s Computer Science Honor Society, as well as Orlando WebCrafters. From the time he wrote out his first equation, he had signed his fate into a world of bytes and binary, to never look back at a time without it.

Harvik Kesineni

Harvik Kesineni is a senior at Orlando Science High School. He started learning about programming and computer science in his 7th-grade computer science class. Since then, he has been working closely with computer science-related activities like his school Robotics Team Clockwork Mania. He is a member of the Computer Science Honor Society and designed websites there. Because of competitions and self-interest, he has learned web development, python, java, and VBA. Building websites is an opportunity for him to test his limits on what he can program and shared interest among his friends.
